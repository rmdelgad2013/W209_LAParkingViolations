Overview

Los Angeles is well-known as a commuter city. WIth the residents’ heavy reliance on cars, it is no surprise that the city also collects a hefty amount of parking ticket revenue - $148 million in fiscal year 2015. The city’s street parking rules can seem like an enigma - often there are three or four posted signs showing seemingly contradictory information on the allowed parking times of any given street.

These tools allow users to determine the best times to park in different areas of Los Angeles, and allows for comparison the fine amounts and the likelihood of getting ticketed in various neighborhoods and streets within the city. We have created several dashboards which allow the user to view different measures across a map of the city. The underlying data is provided by the City of Los Angeles and hosted on Kaggle.

Citation Counts by Zip Dashboard

This view shows the number of citations in any given zip code within Los Angeles. Click on one (or more) of the Violation Description or Day of Week bars in the histograms to filter the map on those categories. You can also filter by zip code by clicking on areas within the map, or using the dropdown filters on the right of the dashboard. Interestingly, you’ll find that while Venice tops the count of parking citations issued during the week, on weekends the neighborhood is not even in the top 20.


Average Fine by Zip Dashboard

This view shows the average citation amount by zip codes of Los Angeles. It is set up with the same filters as the previous dashboard, allowing filters on day of week, violation type, zip code, and neighborhood. Handicap parking violations appear to be the most expensive infraction, at an average of $90.90 per ticket.


Maps Dashboard

This dashboard allows you to zoom in on the filter on zip codes in the zip code map and zoom in on a street view of the area - showing a density plot of the citations in that given zip allowing for a more granular view of where the citation “hot spots” are. For example, one can see that if they were visiting Hollywood, parking near the intersection of Hollywood Blvd and North Wilton Place would be particularly tempting fate.
